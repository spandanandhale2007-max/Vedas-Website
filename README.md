            <h2>The Four Vedas</h2>
            <p>There are four primary Vedas: Rigveda, Yajurveda, Samaveda, and Atharvaveda, each divided into Samhitas (hymns), Brahmanas (ritual explanations), Aranyakas (symbolic rituals), and Upanishads (philosophy).[web:1][web:9]</p>
            <table>
                <tr><th>Veda</th><th>Key Features</th></tr>
                <tr><td>Rigveda</td><td>Oldest Veda with 1,028 hymns praising deities like Indra, Agni; 10 mandalas.[web:3][web:8]</td></tr>
                <tr><td>Yajurveda</td><td>Book of prayers and rituals; includes prose mantras; Shukla and Krishna versions.[web:8]</td></tr>
                <tr><td>Samaveda</td><td>Melodies and chants derived from Rigveda; earliest reference to singing.[web:8]</td></tr>
                <tr><td>Atharva Veda</td><td>730 hymns, magic, charms, daily life procedures; 20 books.[web:8]</td></tr>
            </table>
        </section>
        <section id="epics">
            <h2>Major Epics</h2>
            <p>The two great epics are Ramayana and Mahabharata, which blend history, mythology, and moral teachings.[web:6][web:10]</p>
            <table>
                <tr><th>Epic</th><th>Summary</th></tr>
                <tr><td>Ramayana</td><td>By Valmiki; life of Rama (Vishnu avatar), exile, Sita's abduction by Ravana, rescue; 24,000 couplets in 7 books.[web:11][web:12]</td></tr>
                <tr><td>Mahabharata</td><td>Longest poem; Pandavas vs Kauravas in Kurukshetra war; includes Bhagavad Gita; over 100,000 verses.[web:16][web:20]</td></tr>
            </table>
        </section>
        <section id="puranas">
            <h2>Puranas</h2>
            <p>Puranas are ancient texts with mythology, history, and philosophy; 18 major Puranas focus on gods like Vishnu, Shiva.[web:10][web:17]</p>
            <ul>
                <li>Vishnu Purana: Deeds of Vishnu.[web:17]</li>
                <li>Shiva Purana: Forms of Shiva.[web:10]</li>
                <li>Bhagavata Purana: Life of Krishna.[web:10]</li>
            </ul>
        </section>
        <section id="upanishads">
            <h2>Upanishads</h2>
            <p>Philosophical texts from Vedas' end; discuss Brahman (ultimate reality), Atman (soul), Moksha (liberation); influenced Buddhism, Jainism.[web:7][web:18]</p>
            <p>Key teaching: "Tat Tvam Asi" (Thou art that) from Chandogya Upanishad.[web:7]</p>
